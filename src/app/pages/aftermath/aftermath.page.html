Aftermath
Of the 11th month, on the 11th day, at the 11th hour...the Great War, that ravaged the world for four long bloody years—is over.
Central Powers Germany German Empire

Total participants: 13,000,000

Total casualties: 6,298,040

After the Allied Hundred Days Offensive, Germany, the last of the Central Powers, asked for an armistice. Kaiser Wilhelm II would abdicate later in 1918. And on Nov. 11, the guns on the Western Front would fall silent. After signing the Treaty of Versailles in 1919, Germany would lose 13% of its territory, reduce its Army size to one-hundred thousand, have a nonexistent navy or airforce, and pay massive war reparations. Finally, blaming the war on Germany would ultimately serve as the catalyst for World War II.


Austro-Hungarian Empire Austria-Hungary, Duel Monarchy

Total participants: 7,800,000

Total casualties: 4,820,000

With the instability of the empire and its defeat after the Battle of Vittorio Veneto, Austria-Hungary would collapse. Splitting the empire between Austria, Hungary, Czechoslovakia, and what later would become Yugoslavia. Austria lost its lands to Italy and Allied forces, with Hungary being in worse shape by losing 72% of its territory.


Ottoman Turkey Ottoman Empire

Total participants: 2,600,000

Total casualties: 1,171,844

Joining its allies in their defeat, the Ottoman Empire would lose its lands on the southern tips of the Middle East to the British and France. Furthermore, Turkey continues its struggle, facing its own social unrest and the Entente wishing to take more out of the dying empire. With Turkey fighting and winning the Turkish War of Independence. Creating the modern state of the Republic of Turkey. In 1922.


Bulgaria Kingdom of Bulgaria

Total participants: 1,200,000

Total casualties: 239,890

While being the smallest out of the Central Powers, Bulgaria was instrumental in the capture of Serbia and Montenegro, crippling Romania, and stiff resistance on the Macedonian Front. Carving its way through the Balkans until the bitter end in 1918.


Entente Powers United Kingdom British Empire

Total participants: 8,904,467

Total casualties: 3,205,126

The "Empire Where the Sun Never Sets" leaves the war, not with its head held up high—but stumbling out, bruised. Gaining colonies from the former German and Ottoman Empires at the cost of much of its wealth and youth. And although it would maintain the status of world power, the cracks within the realm were beginning to show. With Britain taking a softener stance as the years go on, well into Nazi Germany and the Axis Powers.


France French Third Republic

Total participants: 8,410,000

Total casualties: 5,663,800

If the United Kingdom came out of the Great War in worse shape than joining in—France was bled white. Having lost over 65% of its fighting force, the 3rd French Republic was somehow able to keep its mutinies during the war under the Germans' noses. Nevertheless, when the Great War ended, France got back Alsace–Lorraine. And was one of the three major powers who decided Germany's harsh fate—with the United States and the United Kingdom.


Tsarist Russia Russian Empire

Total participants: 12,000,000

Total casualties: 6,761,000

Suffering staggering losses in human lives and battles, the Russian Empire would go through two revolutions. Eventually fell to the Communist Bolsheviks in October 1917. And, after the Treaty of Brest-Litovsk would tear Russia into a new war: a civil war.


Italy Kingdom of Italy

Total participants: 5,093,000

Total casualties: 1,416,277

Switching sides in 1915, the Kingdom of Italy was promised large sums of money and territory from the Treaty of London. While Italy would get some of what it was promised by the Entente…it fell far short. Moreover, after losing ⅕ of its Armed Forces and being treated as a minor power at Versailles.


Belgium Kingdom of Belgium

Total participants: 380,000

Total casualties: 58,401

Ending its neutrality by the war's end, the Kingdom of Belgium would demand concessions at Versailles. However, the rewards were mild at best in terms of strategic regions, cash, or colonial expansion.


Serbia Kingdom of Serbia

Total participants: 707,343

Total casualties: 258,148

Being the first in the war, Serbia was far more demanding than Belgium. Demanding more concessions from the war—and succeeding. With the formation of Yugoslavia, by December 1918.


America United States of America

Total participants: 4,743,826

Total casualties: 318,203

Having little influence in 1914…the U.S. had a new sense of importance on the world stage. Helping greatly with the deployment of 2 million soldiers to assist the French and British finally defeating the German Army. Intended to create a league and set of points, the 14 Points, that the world should now move forward with, proposed by U.S. President Woodrow Wilson. However, many of Wilson's 14 Points were ignored by the European Powers of the Allies and Congress, denying America's entry to join the League of Nations.


Imperial Japan Empire of Japan

Total participants: 800,000

Total casualties: 1,207

For their military merit, the Empire of Japan was granted the German colonies and territories it took during the war. From the Marianas to the Marshall Islands. Nevertheless, Japanese representatives at Versailles felt snubbed by the other Allied powers. A seemingly small offensive with grave consequences twenty years later.


Greece Kingdom of Greece

Total participants: 230,000

Total casualties: 26,000

For their effort during the war, Greece was able to gain more territory out of Bulgaria from its southwest territory on the coast. However, being one of the leading powers in the Aegean Sea and promises from the Entente for a piece of the Ottoman Empire—the Greek Army would invade Turkey. Eventually thrown out by Turkish forces, under the command of Atatürk, by 1922.


Romania Kingdom of Romania

Total participants: 750,000

Total casualties: 455,706

Despite having to sign an armistice with the Central Powers in 1917, Romania was able to turn its fortunes with Austria's collapse. Queen Marie of Romania convinced the Entente Powers to grant large chunks of land and recognition. Due to Romania's strategic location and to act as a buffer between Europe and Russia.


Portuguese Portuguese Republic

Total participants: 65,166

Total casualties: 20,973

Spending much of the Portuguese Army on the African continent and some units on the Western Front—Portugal gained the port of Kionga.
