Fronts Western Front Overview:

After the failure of the Schlieffen Plan in 1914, a fortified and long stretch of trench lines was dug. From the shores of the North Sea to the border of Switzerland. Here, the armies of France, Britain, and their allies would fight for four long and bloody years against the German Empire.


Eastern Front Overview:

Squaring off for control of the east. The German Empire and Austria-Hungary battled the Russian Empire and her allies with the longest frontline of the war. Too long that the static lines of trenches were utterly impossible to stay for long.


Balkans Campaign Overview:

In the region that bellowed out the trumpets of war in 1914, nearly all of the Central Powers and Entente fought one way or the other here. A chaotic front that saw the changing of hands between both conflicting sides. That made the Balkan nations—many previously neutral—align with either warring party.


Italian Front Overview:

A land of several hundred to thousand high mountains and slopes, the Alpine Front or Italian Front was far deadlier than the Western Front. The prospect of more significant injury from splinters and debris from artillery shells off the mountainside made the experience abysmal. And finally displayed the carnage both the Kingdom of Italy and the Austro-Hungarian Empire were willing to dish out from their hated feuds that go back to the First Italian War of Independence.


Middle East Campaign Overview:

With the oil that laid under the sands the Ottoman Empire controlled, a conflict of interest would occur and started by 1915 when the Turks entered the war. Battling in the vast and open deserts for supremacy. The Empire of Britain would duel the centuries-old Ottoman Empire under the burning sun and lack of enough water.


Africa Theater Overview:

With German colonies on the African continent, an Allied response to secure these Germanic lands was declared. While most German colonies out in the west and south Africa would fall relatively quickly, German East Africa would continue to fight till the 1918 Armistice.


Asian-Pacific Campaign Overview:

Regulated to taking out German colonies and battling its Imperial Navy, the Entente would secure shipping lanes from the Far East by 1915. Minimal fighting would engulf Asia and the Pacific Ocean.


Naval Campaign Overview:

From all across the seven seas, naval clashes between the two alliances would engulf shipping lanes from the North Sea, with the British Royal Navy and German High Seas Fleet. And too essential shipping lanes as Entente ships fought to maintain necessary foodstuffs and munitions from being sunk by German submarines.
