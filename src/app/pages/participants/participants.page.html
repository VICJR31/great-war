Participants Central Powers Germany German Empire

Joining the war in 1914 in support of its Austrian ally, the German Empire would strike and fight on bitter fronts against the Entente armies. From the fields of Northern France to the frozen steps of Russia. Solidifying Germany’s crucial role against its enemies and leadership amongst the Central Powers.


Austro-Hungarian Empire Austria-Hungary, Duel Monarchy

Declaring war on Serbia for the assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand, heir to the Austrian-Hungarian throne, the multi-ethnic empire poured its efforts into the fight. Suffering initial defeat against the smaller Serbia and far bigger Russia. Austria would show its more remarkable triumph against the Italians along the Dolomites and Isonzo River. Eventually, with the help of Germany, they defeated the Russian Empire and most of the allied armies in the east.


Ottoman Turkey Ottoman Empire

Carrying out a surprise attack on Russia’s Black Sea coast, the Ottoman Empire, dubbed “the Sick Man of Europe,” throws its ancient weight behind Germany and Austria. Turning much of the Allied attention against Turkey’s holding, particularly with the British. Managing to earn its place with the victory against the Allies for Gallipoli. Proved vital in establishing the Republic of Turkey, after the war, by the battle-hearten veterans, such as Mustafa Kemal Atatürk.


Bulgaria Kingdom of Bulgaria

While being the smallest out of the Central Powers, Bulgaria was instrumental in the capture of Serbia and Montenegro, crippling Romania, and stiff resistance on the Macedonian Front. Carving its way through the Balkans until the bitter end in 1918.


Entente Powers United Kingdom British Empire

In response to the German invasion of Belgium, the wrath of the British Empire was brought into the conflict. Expanding the war into a world war as Great Britain’s rule of over ¼ of the world entered on its side. Pouring centuries of wealth, resources, and men against the Central Powers, becoming a leading Allied Power.


France French Third Republic

Bearing a grudge from their defeat in the Franco-Prussian War and its alliance with the Russian Empire, the Republic of France was more than willing to fight against its German enemies. Fighting for its sovereignty in its homeland and throughout the globe for its colonies. Became one of the few nations with an actual motorized army by 1918.


Tsarist Russia Russian Empire

A country with the most considerable territory from Central Europe to the Pacific. When Austria declared war on Serbia on July 28, 1914, the Russian Empire mobilized on July 30, 1914. Two days later, the German Empire declared war on Russia. Although the Tsarist state suffered heavy losses throughout the Great War, the Russian Empire managed to hold its own against all of the Central Powers on the Eastern Front.


Italy Kingdom of Italy

Once being a part of the Triple Alliance with Germany and Austria, the Kingdom of Italy would initially stay neutral, as the pact was only a defensive agreement. However, following the Treaty of London in 1915, the Kingdom of Italy would fight alongside the Entente Powers. Battling its Austrian counterparts in the mountainous terrain of the Alps and taking part in campaigns against its former allies.


Belgium Kingdom of Belgium

The Kingdom of Belgium was perhaps one of the few nations whose country was nearly entirely occupied from 1914 to 1918…and kept fighting to the bitter end. Whether it be in the trenches with their English and French allies. Or in the jungles of Congo against German East Africa. The Belgium Army was always ready for a fight.


Serbia Kingdom of Serbia

One of the first nations to fight, Serbia and its neighbor, Montenegro, would hold off two separate Austrian invasions. Ultimately taking the combined efforts of the Central Powers—and the recently joined Bulgaria—to finally smash the Kingdom of Serbia in 1915. Yet, after reorganizing in neutral Greece, the Serbian Army would fight to eventually liberate its country by the war’s end.


America United States of America

Neutral through most of the Great War, American public opinion would slowly change. Whether it be from the less favorable view of the German invasion of neutral Belgium or the sinking of the Lusitania—a British cruise vessel carrying munitions and Americans on board. America would throw its weight behind the Allied Powers in 1917, following the Zimmerman Telegram. Finally, boosting Entente confidence after the surrender of Russia. Helping to eventually lead the United States down the path of world power and ultimately a superpower by the end of the 2nd World War.


Imperial Japan Empire of Japan

Entering the war a short time after England’s declaration of war against Germany, the Empire of Japan played an essential role in the Far East. Securing the islands near the coast of China and the rest of the Pacific, in conjunction with Entente countries nearby, held by the German Empire.


Greece Kingdom of Greece

Being neutral for the majority of the conflict, the Kingdom of Greece, following King Constantine I—who was more in favor of the Central Powers—abdicating his throne, declared war on the Central Powers. Assisting greatly in the Macedonian Front against the Bulgarians.


Romania Kingdom of Romania

The Kingdom of Romania joined in 1916 on the side of the Entente. Following the successful Russian Brusilov Offensive, Romania diverted many resources from Germany and her allies. However, Romania was decimated in a few short months and faced a temporary occupation once the Russian Empire dropped out of the war and into its civil war.


Portuguese Portuguese Republic

In 1916, following an incident between the Portuguese and a German supply vessel, the German Empire declared war on Portugal. From 1917 to 1918, the PEC (Portuguese Expeditionary Corps) would fight on the Western Front.
